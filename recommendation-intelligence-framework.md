# Recommendation Intelligence™

## A Framework for Understanding AI Recommendations

Recommendation Intelligence™ is the study of how AI systems select, rank, and recommend businesses, products, and brands.

As commerce shifts from search-driven discovery to recommendation-driven discovery, understanding recommendation behavior becomes increasingly important.

Traditional search engines helped users find information.

AI systems increasingly help users make decisions.

This creates a new challenge.

Being visible does not guarantee being recommended.

Being recommended does not guarantee being selected.

Recommendation Intelligence exists to measure and understand this gap.

---

# The Problem

Most businesses currently focus on visibility.

Questions such as:

* Does AI know my brand?
* Am I being cited?
* Am I appearing in AI answers?

are becoming common.

These are important questions.

But they do not explain why one business gets recommended while another does not.

Visibility explains presence.

Recommendation explains preference.

The difference between the two may become one of the most important competitive advantages in AI commerce.

---

# Visibility Is Not Recommendation

A business can be:

* highly visible
* frequently cited
* widely discussed

and still rarely be recommended.

Likewise, a business can receive recommendations despite having weak technical infrastructure, poor machine readability, or low AI Commerce Scores.

Early Recommendation Intelligence research conducted by Atom Foundry across multiple commerce categories suggests that recommendation behavior is often driven by factors that differ from traditional optimization signals.

Understanding those factors is the central objective of Recommendation Intelligence.

---

# The Recommendation Layer

Recommendation Intelligence proposes that AI commerce consists of multiple layers.

## Visibility Layer

Can AI find the business?

## Recommendation Layer

Does AI choose the business?

## Decision Layer

Does the recommendation influence the user's decision?

## Transaction Layer

Does the recommendation lead to a purchase?

Most current AI optimization focuses on visibility.

Recommendation Intelligence focuses on the layer between visibility and transaction.

---

# Core Metrics

## Recommendation Share™

Recommendation Share measures how much of the recommendation market belongs to a brand.

Formula:

Recommendation Share = Brand Recommendations / Total Recommendations

Recommendation Share helps identify which brands dominate AI recommendation behavior within a category.

---

## Recommendation Frequency™

Recommendation Frequency measures how often a brand appears across recommendation opportunities.

Formula:

Recommendation Frequency = Recommendation Appearances / Total Recommendation Opportunities

This metric helps identify recommendation consistency.

---

## Recommendation Position™

Recommendation Position measures where a brand appears within a recommendation list.

Lower positions indicate stronger recommendation preference.

A brand consistently appearing first may have greater recommendation influence than a brand appearing lower in results.

---

## Recommendation Confidence™

Recommendation Confidence measures how strongly an AI system appears to support a recommendation.

Not all recommendations are equal.

Some recommendations are definitive.

Others are uncertain, conditional, or hedged.

Recommendation Confidence attempts to quantify this distinction.

# Recommendation → Decision → Transaction

Recommendation Intelligence assumes that every AI-driven purchase begins with a recommendation.

The recommendation layer influences:

* consideration
* trust
* selection
* purchase probability

Understanding recommendation behavior may therefore become as important as understanding search rankings was during the search era.
